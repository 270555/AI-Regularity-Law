AI Responsibility Act

A proposed law to ensure artificial intelligence is safe, fair, and accountable.

🚨 What Problem Does This Solve?

As AI becomes more powerful, it is increasingly used in hiring, healthcare, law enforcement, and social media. However, there are currently few rules ensuring that these systems are safe or fair.

This law addresses problems such as:

  • Bias and discrimination in AI decisions
  • Lack of accountability when AI causes harm
  • Privacy risks from data collection
  • Unregulated deployment of powerful AI systems

📜 Main Rules of the Law

  • Mandatory Safety Testing: All AI systems must pass safety and bias checks before release.
  • Transparency Requirement: Companies must explain how their AI makes decisions.
  • Accountability: Developers and companies are legally responsible for harm caused by AI.
  • Data Protection: AI systems must respect user privacy and limit data collection.
  • Government Oversight: A regulatory agency will monitor high-risk AI systems.

👥 Who Benefits?

  • Everyday citizens protected from harmful AI decisions
  • Workers impacted by automation
  • Businesses that want clear and fair rules
  • Developers who follow ethical standards

⚠️ Possible Criticisms

  • Could slow down innovation in AI development
  • May increase costs for companies
  • Hard to enforce across different countries

🗳️ Why Voters Should Support It

AI is shaping the future of society. Without regulation, it can harm people, invade privacy, and make unfair decisions.

The AI Responsibility Act ensures that technology works for people—not against them—by making AI safer, more transparent, and accountable.
